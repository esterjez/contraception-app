Trends in Contraceptive Use Among Women in Slovakia (2011–2023)

This line chart shows the number of Slovak women using different types of contraceptive methods at the end of each year between 2011 and 2023. The data is grouped into three main categories: hormonal methods, intrauterine devices (IUDs), and other methods.

Hormonal contraception — such as birth control pills, patches, and injections — remains the most commonly used method. The number of users increased steadily from around 110,000 in 2011 to a peak of nearly 284,000 in 2011, with some fluctuations afterward. This growth may reflect increased accessibility, better awareness, or a greater trust in hormonal methods over time.

Intrauterine device use, though significantly lower in absolute numbers, has also shown a gradual rise. This suggests growing acceptance of long-acting reversible contraceptives, which are often recommended for their effectiveness and low maintenance.

The "other" category includes barrier methods, implants, or less commonly reported methods. While these numbers are the smallest, the trend still indicates slow but consistent growth, suggesting increasing diversification in contraceptive preferences.

These long-term trends highlight how women’s reproductive choices in Slovakia have evolved over time and may reflect broader societal changes in healthcare access, education, and attitudes toward contraception.
